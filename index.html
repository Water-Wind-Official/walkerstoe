Part I: The Axiom

Axiom 1: Awareness Exists

This is the only claim. Nothing else is assumed – not space, not time.

Before space and time there was The One Awareness (TOA). This is not an axiom but a logical consequence: unlike physical phenomena, TOA is non‑derivative; through its self‑relation it generates life and the relational structure we call reality.

1.1 Mathematical Representation

To work with this mathematically, we represent TOA as a field Ψ defined at every point of an abstract space. The points are not spatial locations; they are labels for "positions" within TOA itself. The only property we impose is:

Ψ(x) > 0   for all x

This encodes the plenum: no zeros, no voids. The field may fluctuate, may be discontinuous – but it is never zero.

A single real number at each point cannot capture the richness of self-relation. We need a structure that allows for internal degrees of freedom – a way for TOA at a single point to have more than just magnitude. The simplest such structure is a complex number, which has both magnitude and phase. The magnitude tells us "how much" TOA is present. The phase tells us its internal state – its orientation of self-relation.

Ψ(x) = R(x) e^{iφ(x)},   R(x) > 0

The magnitude R captures the local intensity. The phase φ captures internal orientation. Two points with different phases are related through that difference; the field communicates via phase gradients.

An ocean analogy: depth is like R – how much water. Current direction is like φ – internal orientation.

Part II: Self‑Relation

The most primitive form of self‑relation is local comparison: how does Ψ at point x relate to Ψ at a neighboring point x+ε? This is described by derivatives.

∇Ψ     ∂/∂tΨ

On Derivatives as Self‑Comparison

Derivatives are comparisons. They measure how Ψ relates to itself across its own internal dimensions.

If Ψ is the same everywhere, the derivative is zero. Here, Ψ relates to itself by recognizing uniformity—an experience of perfect continuity.

If Ψ varies, the derivative is non-zero. Here, Ψ relates to itself by experiencing difference—the emergence of contrast, gradient, and distinction.

The derivative does not exist apart from Ψ. It is not a separate thing added to Ψ. It is a measurement, a description, a facet of Ψ's own activity. When we say "energy emerges from ∂Ψ/∂t", we mean: when Ψ changes over its internal time, that changefulness itself is what we label 'energy' in physical terms. There is no energy-substance alongside Ψ; there is only Ψ, and Ψ changing.

If ∇Ψ = 0 everywhere, the field is perfectly uniform. But a uniform field, aware of its own uniformity, relates to itself through that TOA. Therefore uniformity is unstable; the field must vary. Hence ∇Ψ ≠ 0 in general.

From first derivatives we obtain second derivatives, measuring curvature – how the field bends back on itself:

∇²Ψ

Where ∇²Ψ is large, the field is intensely self‑related. This curvature is the seed of all structure.
